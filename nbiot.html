About

Currently, the available infrasture inheritly does not support the conversion to "smart" for objects that cannot afford a $1000 add on with either huge battery packs or connection to the grid for constant electricty. This is because sims and networks were not built for tiny data transmissions that do not require the vast capabilities of luxurious mobile broadband. The creation and standardisation NB-IoT is a nod in agreement from the telecommunications companies and marks the beginning of the real IoT revolution. Under the umbrella of 3GPP radio-access technologies for mobile broadband have evolved effectively to provide connectivity to billions of subscribers and things. Here at IoTa we specialise in lower power, low data transmission IoT applications and research to help make everything smart; leading the transition from M2M communications to thing2thing communications.

NB-IoT Specification and design targets

10yr+ Battery Life

Due to the precise nature of NB-IoT data transmission, converting from analogue to digital and vice vera requires a much smaller bit rate compared to mobile broadband for example. This has led to a 20db bandwidth range being allocated to NB-IoT which infact also increases coverage by upto 7 fold meaning that areas such as basements or rural areas can easily be reached. These are just some of the elements which contribute to the ability of creating an over 10 year battery life application

Low Cost

From the outset, a low unit cost design target was set for NB-IoT to enable mass adoption and integration. With the modem and battery serving as integrated parts of the chip hardware instead of external additions, NB-IoT chips will be available for less than $5 per unit compared to the $10-$40 range for sims alone currently employed in M2M use cases. In addition to the hardware cost, data costs will likely be $0 compared to current data costs of upto $100 per unit in M2M cases. And finally provisioning costs which will be less than $2 compared to over $1000 in some cases for current M2M applications.

Standardasiation

NB-IoT is now the 3GPP radio-technology designed to meet the connectivity requirements for massive MTC applications. The advantages of being part of 3GPP include being part of the LTE ecosystem, leading to fast development, economies of scale, and global roaming. In terms of frameworks and software, they currently exist; simply require additions to help NB-IoT slot in alongside Mobile broadband and other LTE technologies. This avoids huge infrastructure building requirement while promoting coexistence in a regulated and secure forum. And finally, having been standardised by 3GPP, NB-IoT is licensed as a future proof technology unlike other emerging radio frequency technologies.
